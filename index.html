Adryana H

Research assistant and aspiring HCI PhD student.

I am a fourth year student at Clark University in Worcester Massachusetts, majoring in computer science and philosophy. During the summer of 2022, I will be conducting research with Dr. Peter Story on the effectiveness/usability of password managers. I also conduct biology research in the Jack Lab, where I study microbial interactions in a variety of plants.

I am broadly interested in designing eco-friendly, robust accessibility technologies that accommodate a broad range of audiences. More specifically, I am interested in studying ways that both robotics and IoT (Internet of Thing) devices can help individuals with disabilites; I am especially fascinated with ubiquitous computing technologies and usable privacy and security. I am also interested in designing technologies that better accommodate cognitively disabled people in virtual spaces, whether that be through UI/UX (user interface/experience) design or AR (augmented reality) technologies. Improving usability in computing systems is my ultimate research goal.
