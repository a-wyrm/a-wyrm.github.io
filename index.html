About Me

I am a third-year PhD student with the GWUSEC Lab at The George Washington University, where I am advised by Adam Aviv. I'm broadly interested in understanding how both physical and digital security devices are used by people with disabilities, and how they can be designed more universally. Other issues in usable security, such as authentication, surveillance, and human factors in S&P adoption are also things I'm interested in.
I recieved my undergraduate degree from Clark University, where I majored in Computer Science and Philosophy. During my undergrad, I conducted research with Peter Story on the usability of password managers.
