Adryana H

Research assistant and aspiring HCI PhD student.

I am a fourth year student at Clark University in Worcester Massachusetts, majoring in computer science and philosophy. During the Summer/Fall of 2022, I am conducting research with Dr. Peter Story on usable security and the effectiveness/usability of password managers. I also work with the Echolab at VT, doing research in crowdsourcing and building conversational assistant software. I previously conducted biology research in the Jack Lab, where I studied growth rates in wheat and clover plants.

I am broadly interested in designing robust accessibility technologies that accommodate a broad range of audiences. More specifically, I am interested in studying ways that both physical (robot and IoT) and digital (password policies, etc.) security apparatuses can become more user-friendly and accessible to people with disabilities. Other issues in security systems, such as human factors in security adoption and privacy in marginalized communities, are also things I'm heavily interested in.

Improving the usability of security is my ultimate research goal.
