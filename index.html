Adryana H

PhD student at GWU.

I am a PhD canadiate at GWU, being advised by Adam Aviv with the GWUSEC Lab. I recieved my undergraduate degree at Clark University, where I majored in Computer Science and Philosophy. During my undergrad, I conducted research with Dr. Peter Story on usable security and password managers.

I'm broadly interested in understanding how universal design principles can be applied to security systems. More specifically, I'm interested in looking at how both physical and digital security apparatuses are used by people with disabilities, and how they can be more accessible. Other issues in usable security, such as human factors in security adoption, privacy in marginalized communities, and collaborative security/privacy, are also things I'm interested in.
